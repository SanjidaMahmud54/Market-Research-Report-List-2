<p><p>The bicycle valve market is primarily divided into two types: rubber tire valves and metal tire valves. Rubber tire valves, typically lighter and more flexible, are commonly found in tubeless systems and are cost-effective. They provide good airtightness but may wear out faster. In contrast, metal tire valves are more durable and resistant to damage, making them suitable for high-performance bikes. These valves can also enhance aesthetics and provide better sealing, appealing to both casual riders and enthusiasts.</p></p>
