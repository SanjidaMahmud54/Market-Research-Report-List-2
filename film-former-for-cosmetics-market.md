<p><p>In the cosmetics market, film formers are categorized into two main types: water-soluble and oil-soluble. Water-soluble film formers create a flexible, breathable film when applied, often used in products like aqueous foundations and skincare. They enhance adhesion while allowing moisture retention. Oil-soluble film formers, on the other hand, provide a more waterproof finish, commonly found in formulations like long-lasting lipsticks and mascaras. Each type serves unique functions to enhance product performance and consumer experience.</p></p>
