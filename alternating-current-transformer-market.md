<p><ul><li>Ultra High Voltage (UHV)</li><li>Extra High Voltage (EHV)</li><li>High Voltage (HV)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The alternating-current transformer market can be categorized into three main types based on voltage levels: Ultra High Voltage (UHV), Extra High Voltage (EHV), and High Voltage (HV). UHV transformers operate at voltages above 800 kV, facilitating long-distance power transmission with minimal losses. EHV transformers range from 345 kV to 800 kV, catering to regional transmission needs. HV transformers function at 100 kV to 345 kV, supporting industrial and commercial applications. Each type plays a crucial role in optimizing electrical grid efficiency and reliability.</p></p>
